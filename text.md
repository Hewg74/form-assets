## Detrimental / Impactful Variants

### MTHFR C677T (rs1801133)
**Result:** `AG` (Heterozygous)  
**Impact:** Moderate Reduction in Methylation (30-40%)
The MTHFR gene produces an enzyme necessary for converting folate (Vitamin B9) into its active form (Methylfolate).
-   **Effect:** You have one copy of the "risk" allele (A) and one normal allele (G). This reduces the enzyme's efficiency by approximately 30-40%.
-   **Consequences:** Potential for slightly elevated homocysteine levels and reduced methylation capacity. This can affect detoxification, neurotransmitter synthesis, and DNA repair.
-   **Interventions:**
    1.  **Supplement with Methylated B-Vitamins:** Look for **L-5-Methyltetrahydrofolate (5-MTHF)** instead of Folic Acid. Avoid synthetic Folic Acid in fortified foods where possible.
    2.  **Support Riboflavin (Vitamin B2):** MTHFR requires B2 as a cofactor.
    3.  **Eat Folate-Rich Foods:** Leafy greens (spinach, kale), asparagus, and avocado.

### COMT V158M (rs4680)
**Result:** `AG` (Heterozygous)
**Impact:** Balanced "Warrior/Worrier"
The COMT gene breaks down neurotransmitters like dopamine, epinephrine, and norepinephrine.
-   **Effect:** You have the "intermediate" activity genotype. You are likely balanced between high dopamine (good for focus, susceptible to anxiety/stress) and lower dopamine (good stress handling, lower baseline focus).
-   **Consequences:** You likely handle stress moderately well but may become overwhelmed if methylation is impaired (since COMT requires SAMe from the methylation cycle).
-   **Interventions:**
    1.  **Magnesium:** COMT is Magnesium-dependent. Ensure adequate Magnesium intake (Glycinate or Threonate forms are best).
    2.  **Manage Stress:** Since you have MTHFR issues, ensure your methylation is supported to keep COMT functioning well.
